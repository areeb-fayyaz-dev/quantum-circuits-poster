Key Quantum Concepts

🔄 Superposition

A qubit can exist in a superposition of both 0 and 1 states simultaneously, unlike classical bits. This is achieved using quantum gates like the Hadamard gate. When measured, it collapses to either 0 or 1 with equal probability.

🔗 Entanglement

Qubits can become entangled, meaning their states are interdependent. Measuring one qubit instantly determines the state of the other, regardless of distance. Einstein called this "spooky action at a distance."

⚛️ Quantum Gates

Hadamard (H): Creates superposition
Pauli-X: Flips qubit state (like classical NOT)
CNOT: Controlled-NOT, creates entanglement
Measure: Collapses qubit to definite state

📊 Measurement & Collapse

When a quantum system is measured, it collapses from a superposition into a definite state. This is fundamental to quantum mechanics and makes quantum computing both powerful and probabilistic.
